THESIS / RESEARCH — 2026

Effect of Joint Loading Angle on Glenoid Baseplate Micromotion

Undergraduate thesis testing how the direction of joint loading affects micromotion at the bone–implant interface in Reverse Shoulder Arthroplasty — the key factor determining whether a baseplate achieves long-term osseointegration or loosens. Used an anatomically correct Sawbones scapula model and LVDT instrumentation to compare loading along the scapular plane versus the transverse scapular plane.

  • Mean micromotion, 0°17.216 µm
  • Mean micromotion, 90°121.047 µm
  • SignificanceWelch's t-test, p < 0.0001
  • SupervisorsDr. Tim Bryant, Dr. Heidi Ploeg
TEAM DESIGN — MECH 462, 2026

Niagara Foot M3V01

A team re-engineering effort on a prosthetic foot adapter, done in collaboration with DOI Ortho-Innovativ to improve access to high-functioning prosthetics in low-resourced regions. Iterated on a potted adapter insert design, developed 3D-printed tooling for manufacturing, and validated the approach through MTS displacement-controlled testing.

  • GoalMatch Niagara Foot Model 2 performance at 30% of prior cost
  • MethodPolyurethane potting, 3D-printed tooling, MTS testing
  • Team5-person design team, faculty advisor Dr. Tim Bryant
RESEARCH / MATLAB

Unilateral Load Carrying & Trunk Kinematics

Marker-based motion capture study tracking shoulder obliquity and trunk lean while walking with a one-sided load, aimed at understanding the postural strain of carrying an infant on one side and informing ways to reduce it. Built a full MATLAB pipeline to clean marker trajectories, segment gait cycles, and compare loading conditions.

  • Built withMATLAB, QTM motion capture data
  • MetricsShoulder obliquity, trunk lean, normalized gait cycle
  • ConditionsNo weight, left-hand load, right-hand load

Exploded View Gearbox
